CLASS REFLECTION 04/03/26

1. I believe a piece of e-literature has to do with what can't be done in print. The interactivity of it, how it can use sound, the visual aspects to it; all things unique to the medium. 
2. My favourite device might have been the one used in First Draft of the Revolution, however it wasn't my favourite experience. Shade was something I'd seen before but it did the choose-your-own-adventure trope in a clever way. As Cris said you can't just do whatever you want, there are semi-strict commands that must be entered to move the story foward. I got a lot more 'That's not a verb I recognize.' than any interesting story. It was funnish. As discussed in class, more a puzzle than a literary experience. I like FDOTR for the device - as it's interactive while still being very literary - but I wasn't into the story. I might use the mechanics of it for my project - the way you can change the letter based on how you want the character to convey themselves. It's a bit Telltale, if you know those games: 'will they like what I've written or not?'.
3. I don't expect, more _want_, to be a coding wizard (hah) but to most importantly create a digital writing project that I'm proud of. I already have lofty ideas based on the examples we've been looking at in class and those referenced in the readings. Looking forward to maybe advancing my career through this class as Jenny Hedley had.
